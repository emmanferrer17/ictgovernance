Advisories & Guidance

Essential information on reporting cyber incidents and detecting fraudulent activities.

How to Report Identity Theft or Hacking

Victims should act immediately to preserve evidence and engage the correct agency.

Step 1: Document & Preserve Evidence
  • Take screenshots of malicious messages, hacked profiles, or unauthorized transactions.
  • Save all email headers, URLs of fake websites, and transaction reference numbers.
Step 2: Immediate Containment
  • For Bank/Financial Theft: Contact your bank immediately to freeze accounts or credit cards.
  • For Social Media Hacking: Use the platform's internal "Report Hacked Account" tools and inform your contacts to prevent further scams.
Step 3: Official Reporting
  • CICC Action Line: Call 1326 (National Anti-Scam Hotline) for immediate technical guidance.
  • NBI Cybercrime Division: Email ccd@nbi.gov.ph or visit the NBI Building in Manila for large-scale fraud or complex cases.
  • PNP Anti-Cybercrime: Report via "E-Sumbong" portal at pnpacg.ph or call (632) 8723-0401.
  • NPC: If personal data was leaked, report via the DBNMS at privacy.gov.ph within 72 hours.

Guidance on Detecting Fake Websites or Investment Scams

Educate yourself to "Think, Examine, and Study" before sharing information or investing.

A. Spotting Fake/Phishing Websites
  • Check the URL: Look for misspellings (e.g., banc0.com instead of banco.com.ph) or unusual domain extensions.
  • Security Protocol: Ensure the site uses https:// and shows a padlock icon, though note that some sophisticated fake sites now use these as well.
  • Urgency & Threats: Beware of sites that use "Fear-of-Missing-Out" (FOMO) or threats like "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours" to force a login.
  • Generic Content: Fake sites often have poor grammar, low-resolution logos, or broken links in the footer.
B. Identifying Investment Scams
  • Unrealistic Returns: If an investment promises "guaranteed" high returns with "zero risk," it is likely a Ponzi or pyramid scheme.
  • Deepfake Promotions: Be cautious of videos featuring celebrities or government officials endorsing platforms; these are often AI-generated "deepfakes".
  • Lack of SEC Registration: Always verify if a company has a secondary license to solicit investments from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).
  • Pressure Tactics: Scammers often pressure you to "invest now" before the opportunity disappears.
