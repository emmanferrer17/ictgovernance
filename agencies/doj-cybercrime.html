Introduction

Created under the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012, the OOC serves as the central authority on all matters related to international mutual assistance and extradition for cyber-related offenses.

It acts as the legal "prosecution arm" for digital crimes, working closely with law enforcement agencies to bring offenders to justice.

Key Responsibilities
1
Focal Agency

Act as the focal agency for investigating and prosecuting cybercrime nationwide.

2
Digital Evidence

Issue preservation orders to service providers to secure digital evidence.

3
International Cooperation

Facilitate international cooperation with law enforcement on cybercrime intelligence.

4
Law Enforcement Strategies

Lead the implementation of law enforcement strategies to curb online fraud and abuse.
