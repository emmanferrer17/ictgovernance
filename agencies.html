Agency Overview

DICT

Department of Information and Communications Technology

The DICT is the primary policy-making and administrative entity of the Executive Branch responsible for planning, developing, and promoting the national ICT development agenda.

The logo features 17 dots representing the 17 regions of the Philippines connected by technology. The radiating lines symbolize frequency waves and the "pulsating" nature of ICT, while the binary numbers around the sphere represent the acronym "DICT" and "2016" (the year it was established).

DOJ – CTF

Department of Justice – Cybercrime Task Force

The DOJ - Office of Cybercrime (OOC) is the lead government agency responsible for implementing the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 (R.A. 10175).

The DOJ seal features a balanced scale (symbolizing equality) over a sword (symbolizing authority in the dispensation of justice), set against a map of the Philippines. The Latin maxim "Justitiae Pax Opus" means "Justice is the work of peace."

NBI – CCD

National Bureau of Investigation – Cybercrime Division

The NBI - Cybercrime Division (CCD) serves as the primary law enforcement unit investigating computer-related, internet-facilitated, and high-tech crimes in the Philippines.

The NBI badge represents the bureau's identity as the premier investigative agency. The inclusion of digital forensic elements in their modern branding signifies their shift toward technical intelligence and "cutting-edge" forensic analysis.
View Full Profile

NPC

National Privacy Commission

The National Privacy Commission (NPC) is an independent body mandated to administer and implement the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA).

The shield design represents the protection and safeguarding of the fundamental human right to privacy. The blue and gold colors symbolize integrity and the authority of the commission as a regulatory body.
